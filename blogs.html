    My visit to Kuthibari was a calm and enriching experience that allowed me to connect with history, literature, and nature at the same time. Kuthibari, located in Shilaidaha, Kushtia, is closely associated with Rabindranath Tagore, and walking through the place felt like stepping into a quieter, more thoughtful era. The main building stood gracefully beside the Padma River, surrounded by greenery that created a peaceful atmosphere. Inside the museum, I saw Tagoreâ€™s personal belongings, handwritten letters, photographs, and furniture, which helped me understand his life beyond textbooks. Each room told a story of creativity, solitude, and deep observation of human emotions. The riverbank was especially memorable; the slow flow of the Padma and the open sky made it easy to imagine how Tagore found inspiration there. I spent some time sitting quietly, reflecting on how nature influences art and thought. The entire tour felt unhurried, allowing me to absorb the surroundings without distraction. Overall, the Kuthibari tour was not just a sightseeing trip but a reflective journey that deepened my appreciation for Bengali literature, history, and the simple beauty of nature.
